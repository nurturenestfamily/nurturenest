📋 Which Car Seat Do You Need?

1

Infant Car Seat (0–12 months)

Rear-facing only. Designed for newborns up to 30–35 lbs. Comes with a base that stays in the car. Best for: newborns to about 9–12 months.

2

Convertible Car Seat (newborn–toddler)

Rear-facing then forward-facing. Grows with your child from 5–65+ lbs. Best value long-term — skip the infant seat entirely if budget is tight.

3

All-in-One Car Seat (newborn–120 lbs)

The ultimate investment. One seat from birth through booster stage. Premium price but saves money in the long run.

4

Booster Seat (4–12 years)

For kids who've outgrown their harness. Positions the seatbelt correctly across the body. Required in most states until age 8.

🛡️ Car Seat Safety Buying Guide

📐

Always Rear-Face as Long as Possible

The AAP recommends rear-facing until your child reaches the seat's maximum height or weight limit — usually age 2–4.

🔧

Installation is Everything

73% of car seats are installed incorrectly. Choose a seat that's easy to install — the safest seat is the one installed correctly.

📅

Check the Expiration Date

All car seats expire (usually 6–10 years). Never use an expired seat or a seat that has been in a crash.

🚗

Check Your Car Fit

Some seats are very wide or deep. Always check that your chosen seat fits properly in your specific vehicle before buying.
