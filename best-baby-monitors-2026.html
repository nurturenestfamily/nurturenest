🛒 Baby Monitor Buying Guide

📡

WiFi vs Non-WiFi

Non-WiFi monitors are more secure and reliable at home. WiFi monitors let you check baby from anywhere via your phone. Consider a dual-mode monitor for the best of both.

🔋

Battery Life

Budget monitors get 6–8 hours. Our top picks get 22–30 hours. Look for at least 10 hours if you use it unplugged at night.

💳

Watch Out for Subscriptions

Some monitors (like Nanit) charge $5–10/month for basic features. Over 2 years that's $240 extra. Our top picks have zero subscription fees.

🏠

Range & Home Size

For large homes, look for 1,000+ foot range. For smaller homes, budget monitors work great. WiFi monitors work anywhere with internet.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best baby monitor overall?

The Infant Optics DXR-8 Pro is our top pick for most parents. It's reliable, secure (no WiFi hacking risk), and has no subscription fees. For parents who want health tracking, the Owlet Dream Duo 2 is worth the extra cost.

Are WiFi baby monitors safe?

WiFi monitors can be vulnerable if not secured. Always use a strong password, keep firmware updated, and choose brands with good security track records. If security is a concern, go with a non-WiFi monitor like the Infant Optics or HelloBaby.

How far should a baby monitor be from the crib?

Position the camera at least 3 feet from the crib, on a high shelf or wall mount, with all cords completely out of baby's reach.

When can I stop using a baby monitor?

Most parents stop using monitors between ages 2–4, depending on their child's independence and the family's comfort level.
